our mission

Our goal is to replenish the Yemeni coffee industry, restoring the hope for its people especially the minorities. We aim to work with and support small farm families to ensure the best quality produce. We establish real partnerships with farmers, educate them on how to grow specialty coffee, and share our proceeds directly with them. We are proud of our farmers as they are of their harvest.

it all started over 500 years ago

Yemen coffee is the ancestral wild coffee beverage which 90-100% of all other coffees we drink today originate from. Since its recent ability to be exported, it has become incredibly sought after and praised for being one of the best coffees in the world.

Most agree that the original coffee plants were native to the western regions of Ethiopia. Coffee was recorded as a beverage as early as the 6th century, utilised by the Ottoman Empire. It was in Yemen, however, that these plants were finally cultivated and developed into the beans and beverage that we know today. History records Sufi monasteries on the southern tip of the Arabian Peninsula processing Yemen coffee over 500 years ago. The Yemen farmers to advantage of the unique terrain of their country, which has conditions of climate and environment which were not considered ideal growing conditions for other plants. For 200 years, Yemen was the only source of coffee. Originally the primary mode of travel was by camel, but later, this Arabic Coffee was named after the Mokha Port which is on the coast of the Red Sea, from which these were eventually shipped.
